# 1. Brand at a glance

BCBSKS materials should feel local, trusted, warm, clear and practical.
The visual system puts Kansas first, while Plain Talk keeps content
understandable and useful for the people we serve.

## Non-negotiables

| **Check**      | **Use this**                                                                                                                                 | **Avoid this**                                                                                                          |
|----------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Name**       | First reference: Blue Cross and Blue Shield of Kansas (BCBSKS). Later references: BCBSKS, Blue Cross, the Kansas company or the company.     | Blue Cross Blue Shield of Kansas; Blue Cross/Blue Shield of Kansas; BC/BS of Kansas; possessive forms such as BCBSKS's. |
| **Logo**       | Use official prepared logo artwork only. Keep the symbols and name in approved configurations.                                               | Recreating, stretching, cropping, adding effects, removing marks or placing the logo on busy/low-contrast backgrounds.  |
| **Disclosure** | Include the licensee disclosure when company name appears. Use full disclosure for co-branding.                                              | Using the short disclosure with unrelated partner/co-branded pieces.                                                    |
| **Color**      | Lead with Blue, Midnight, Sky/Bluejay and approved grays. Use no more than three colors when possible.                                       | Using Warning Red as a brand color; using Honeybee/Marigold as small text; overusing accents.                           |
| **Copy**       | Use Plain Talk: audience-first, conversational, active, concise and easy to scan.                                                            | Dense paragraphs, insurance jargon, all caps sentences, justified text or unexplained acronyms.                         |
| **Review**     | Use Corporate Communications review for broad internal or external distribution. Confirm legal/brand needs when public-facing or co-branded. | Skipping review when sending to large audiences or creating external materials.                                         |

## Brand personality for internal training and enablement

- **Clear over clever:** Use headings, short sections, examples and
  action steps.

- **Helpful over decorative:** Every color, icon, screenshot or callout
  should help someone act.

- **Kansas-first:** Use local, community-centered language and visuals
  when appropriate.

- **Human and approachable:** Prefer warm, honest moments; use real
  member/community imagery when possible.

- **Structured:** Give people the path: what it is, why it matters, what
  to do next, where to get help.

# 2. Logo system

The logo system includes symbols and the company name. Use only official
artwork and approved configurations. The Blue Cross Blue Shield
Association requirements protect the symbols, signature and licensee
disclosure.

<img src="/mnt/data/brandkit_media/media/image1.png"
style="width:4.6in;height:0.77939in" />

## Primary logo

- Use for most internal and external Blue Cross branded communications.

- Required elements: the symbols and the name.

- Two-color version combines Pantone 300 Blue Cross Blue symbols with
  black type.

- Minimum size: 2 inches wide.

- Clear space: at least the height of the Blue Cross symbol around the
  mark.

## Secondary logo

<img src="/mnt/data/brandkit_media/media/image2.png"
style="width:4.3in;height:0.65088in" />

- Use when the full wordmark may be illegible at scale, when the mark
  appears briefly on screen, or when the primary mark already appears
  elsewhere.

- Minimum height: 0.25 inches.

- Safe space and licensee disclosure rules still apply.

- Useful for digital placements, social posts, email contexts and
  out-of-home placements where speed and legibility matter.

## Tertiary logo

- Use the stacked mark when horizontal space is limited.

- Preferred treatment: two-color version for the closest connection to
  the core logo.

- Minimum width: 1 inch.

- Useful for promotional items, apparel, embroidery, cylindrical items
  and narrow spaces.

## Licensee disclosure

Full version: Blue Cross and Blue Shield of Kansas is an independent
licensee of the Blue Cross Blue Shield Association.

Short version for approved scenarios: An independent licensee of the
Blue Cross Blue Shield Association.

Co-branded materials require the full disclosure and a relationship
statement. The BCBSKS logo should be more prominent than the unrelated
entity logo and placed above or to the left of it.

## Logo misuse to avoid

- Do not recreate the logo or build a new version from text and symbols.

- Do not stretch, compress, skew, crop, rearrange or add effects.

- Do not remove registration marks from the logo artwork.

- Do not place the logo on photography, color fields or patterns that
  reduce legibility.

- Do not recolor the symbols except through approved treatments.

## Color rules of thumb

- Try not to use more than three colors on one piece.

- Choose colors that complement imagery instead of fighting it.

- Use Midnight at 80% tint when used as a large flood so it does not
  become too dark.

- Avoid Honeybee or Marigold for small text, especially in projected
  presentations.

- Use Text Gray instead of 100% black for body text. The logo uses 100%
  black where specified.

- Use Subtle Gray for callouts. Use White to keep layouts digestible and
  aligned with Plain Talk.

- Check contrast for digital applications before publishing.

# 4. Typography

Use the official marketing fonts when available. For everyday
employee-created communications, use accessible, broadly available
system fonts from the approved substitutions.

| **Use case**            | **Headline / display** | **Body / support**                                          | **Notes**                                                                                      |
|-------------------------|------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Marketing materials** | Mencken STD Extra Bold | Univers 57 Condensed / Univers family                       | Core brand fonts; not available to most departments.                                           |
| **Letters**             | Georgia as needed      | Georgia; Arial may be used for member-facing correspondence | Corporate letterhead should be used for professional correspondence.                           |
| **PowerPoint**          | Georgia Bold           | Arial Narrow                                                | Built into the BCBSKS PowerPoint template. Sample headline size: 24 pt; body copy often 12 pt. |
| **Email**               | Aptos                  | Aptos                                                       | Default email font. CT Suite may use a default font such as Calibri.                           |
| **General documents**   | Georgia or Arial       | Georgia or Arial                                            | Minimum 11 pt; senior market letters and solicitation materials must be 12 pt or larger.       |

## Formatting principles

- Use sentence case for most content. Title case may be used selectively
  for design clarity.

- Use bold instead of all caps for emphasis. Reserve bold for important
  phrases or keywords.

- Use italics only for emphasis, quotes, names of publications and
  conversations.

- Never double space after punctuation. Use one space.

- Do not use justified margins for Plain Talk content.

## Training asset typography pattern

- Page title: Georgia Bold or Arial Bold, 18-24 pt depending on format.

- Section headings: Georgia Bold or Arial Bold, 12-16 pt.

- Body: Arial or Arial Narrow, 11-12 pt minimum.

- Callouts: Bold lead phrase + short sentence. Keep callouts useful, not
  decorative.

- Screenshots: Add numbered Bluejay markers or clear labels. Do not
  over-label the screen.

# 5. Visual language

## Patterns

- Use patterns as accents to add warmth and a handmade feel.

- Keep patterns from overpowering the information.

- Avoid placing patterns underneath body copy or over the subject of a
  photo.

- Use one pattern per page for clarity.

- Set pattern opacity between 15% and 20%, depending on color and
  complexity.

- Use same-color shade pairings. Approved exceptions include 20% gray
  over beige, Sky Blue over Blue and 20% gray over Sky Blue.

## Photography

- Choose warm, honest moments that reflect life as it happens.

- Use well-lit images with natural light.

- Subjects should appear relaxed, friendly and approachable.

- Use real members when possible. Avoid stock photos and low-quality
  images when better options exist.

## Illustration

- Use illustrations when photography is not available or appropriate.

- Use them sparingly and intentionally.

- Use the Kansas color palette to add warmth.

- Show diversity.

- Use one illustration per layout.

- If a circle is used as a background element, the illustration may
  extend beyond the circle.

## Charts and graphs

- Prioritize readability. Keep charts simple and concise.

- Use brand grays and blues first.

- Use a pop of color only to call out a specific data point.

- For five data points, use Blue tints at 100%, 70%, 50%, 30% and 10%.
  Above five data points, use grays.

- Use superscript percentage symbols in large callouts; use text-height
  percentages in text and bar graphs.

# 6. Voice, tone and Plain Talk

Plain Talk is the writing standard for reducing complexity and improving
the reader experience. It matters especially in health insurance, where
acronyms and complex concepts can create friction.

## Four principles

1.  Use language your audience can easily understand.

2.  Write in a conversational style, as if you were speaking.

3.  Organize content with your readers' needs in mind. What does the
    member need to know?

4.  Use reader-friendly formatting so the information looks easy to
    read.

## Plain Talk checklist

- Aim for a sixth- to eighth-grade reading level.

- Choose common, everyday words. Avoid insurance and medical jargon when
  possible.

- Define terms the reader might not know. Add an example, analogy or
  visual aid when helpful.

- Use active voice and first person: I, we and you.

- Keep sentences short. Average 15 words or fewer when possible.

- Limit paragraphs to one main idea. One or two sentences can be enough.

- Use clear, descriptive headings that act as road signs.

- Put the most important information first. Use the inverted pyramid.

- Use bullets, numbered steps and FAQs to make content easy to scan.

- Use at least 30% white space. Keep top/bottom margins at least 1 inch
  and side margins at least 1.25 inches.

- Use Georgia or Arial at 11 pt or larger; use larger type for special
  populations when needed.

- Read the copy aloud, ask someone unfamiliar with the project to review
  it, and double-check names, links, emails and phone numbers.

# 7. Corporate writing style

## Company name

- **Correct first reference:** Blue Cross and Blue Shield of Kansas
  (BCBSKS).

- **Acceptable later references:** BCBSKS, Blue Cross, the Kansas
  company or the company.

- **Do not write:** Blue Cross Blue Shield of Kansas; Blue Cross/Blue
  Shield of Kansas; Kansas Blue Cross and Blue Shield; BC/BS of Kansas;
  BlueCross and BlueShield of Kansas.

- **Do not use possessive forms:** Avoid BCBSKS's, BCBSKS', Blue Cross',
  Blue Cross and Blue Shield of Kansas's. Rewrite the sentence.

- **Do not write Cross and Shield alone:** Use Blue before each word
  when referring to Blue Cross and Blue Shield.

## Capitalization

- Avoid unnecessary capitals.

- Use sentence case for most content.

- Capitalize official department, division and committee names.

- Capitalize job titles when they come before a name or when used after
  the name as a description. Lowercase titles used without a name.

- Capitalize product names and terms specifically listed in the
  Corporate Style Guide.

- Do not capitalize seasons, claim form, enrollment form, primary care
  physician, preferred provider organization or point-of-service unless
  part of an official name.

## Common high-risk terms

| **Term**            | **Rule**                                                   |
|---------------------|------------------------------------------------------------|
| **bcbsks.com**      | Always lowercase.                                          |
| **BlueAccess®**     | One word; capitalize A; registered trademark on first use. |
| **Blue365®**        | One word; registered trademark on first use.               |
| **BlueCard®**       | One word; uppercase C.                                     |
| **BlueConnect**     | One word; capitalize C; employee SharePoint intranet.      |
| **Coinsurance**     | One word; no hyphen.                                       |
| **Copay/copayment** | One word; no hyphen.                                       |
| **Health care**     | Two words unless part of an official external name.        |
| **Level-funded**    | Hyphenated.                                                |
| **Payer**           | Use payer, not payor.                                      |
| **Preventive**      | Use preventive, not preventative.                          |
| **Value-based**     | Hyphenated.                                                |
| **Well-being**      | Hyphenated.                                                |

## Dates, times and numbers

| **Check**              | **Use this**                                                                                                                  | **Avoid this**                                                              |
|------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Months**             | Spell out months alone or with year alone. Abbreviate Jan., Feb., Aug., Sept., Oct., Nov. and Dec. only with a specific date. | Do not add -st, -nd, -rd or -th to dates.                                   |
| **Events**             | Use time-date-location order: 10 a.m., Friday, Oct. 8, in Building K.                                                         | Do not use on or held unnecessarily with days and dates.                    |
| **Time**               | Use lowercase a.m. and p.m. with periods. Omit :00 in running copy unless agenda/list consistency requires minutes.           | AM/PM; 8:00 a.m. in normal running copy when 8 a.m. works.                  |
| **Numbers**            | Write out one through nine. Use numerals for 10 and above. Use numerals in charts/lists when useful.                          | Starting a sentence with a numeral.                                         |
| **Money**              | Use \$4, \$25, \$500, \$1,000, \$650,000, \$1 million, \$1.5 million.                                                         | Writing cents with a symbol in running text when the word cents is clearer. |
| **Digital directions** | Italicize interface text: select Review \> Check Document.                                                                    | Vague directions or unformatted button/tab names.                           |

# 8. Templates and channel application

## Letters and correspondence

- Use corporate letterhead for professional correspondence.

- Use AICK or ASK letterhead for subsidiary correspondence when
  applicable.

- Use Georgia for letters; member-facing departments may use Arial.

- Left-justified format is preferred.

- Align the left margin under the far-left side of the logo and the
  right margin with the far-right side of the logo.

- Do not place code numbers, salutations or addresses above the
  letterhead header.

- Use letterhead only on the first page of multi-page letters.

- Use white paper for official correspondence.

- Export letterhead Word documents to PDF before emailing outside
  contacts.

## PowerPoint

- Use the BCBSKS PowerPoint template.

- Use Georgia Bold for headlines and Arial Narrow for remaining text.

- Template examples include section slides, content slides, color
  swatches, table styles, chart examples, callout slides, quote slides
  and closing slides.

- Use Blue, Midnight, Sky, Bluejay and grays as the default presentation
  palette. Use Warning Red only for warnings or negative financial
  numbers.

- Keep slides uncluttered; one key message per slide is the safest
  default.

## Email

- Use Aptos as the default email font.

- Keep subject lines specific and action-oriented.

- Lead with the action or key point in the first two lines.

- Use bullets for steps, deadlines or choices.

- Use the standard company email signature.

## SharePoint and digital content

- Use descriptive page titles and headings.

- Keep one idea per section or page where possible.

- Use bullets, FAQs and short paragraphs for scanning.

- Check contrast and avoid low-contrast text over imagery or patterned
  backgrounds.

- Make the next step obvious: button, link, contact, deadline or support
  path.

## Training and enablement assets

| **Check**                 | **Use this**                                                                            | **Avoid this**                                                       |
|---------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Quick reference guide** | One page, short sections, 3-5 steps, screenshots or icons only where they help.         | Long policy language or multiple dense tables.                       |
| **SOP**                   | Purpose, audience, prerequisites, procedure, exceptions, support path and revision log. | Unnumbered steps or buried decision points.                          |
| **Job aid**               | Start with the task, then the minimum steps needed to complete it.                      | Background-heavy introductions.                                      |
| **Slide deck**            | One message per slide, clean hierarchy, template fonts and approved colors.             | Too many colors, tiny type or decorative patterns behind content.    |
| **Screenshot guide**      | Use fake/member-safe data. Highlight only target controls. Keep callouts simple.        | Real personal information, cluttered labels or low-contrast markers. |

# 9. Review and approval path

Use the approval path early when a piece is public-facing, co-branded,
high-distribution, complex, legally sensitive or likely to be reused as
a template.

## When to route for review

- External/public materials.

- Internal materials distributed to 75 or more people.

- Materials using the logo, co-branding, partner references or legal
  disclosure language.

- Member-facing content or content that explains benefits, claims,
  costs, coverage or health care terms.

- Templates that other teams will reuse.

- Materials where the newest source guidance is unclear or conflicting.

## Review contacts from source materials

| **Need**                                                          | **Contact / route**       |
|-------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------|
| **External/public communication questions**                       | externalcomms@bcbsks.com  |
| **Internal communication questions**                              | corpcomm@bcbsks.com       |
| **Logo placement / graphic standards / brand creative questions** | bcreative@bcbsks.com      |
| **Legal issues concerning brands**                                | legal.services@bcbsks.com |

## Pre-submit checklist

- Correct company name on first reference.

- Approved logo file and enough clear space.

- Required licensee disclosure included where needed.

- Palette limited and contrast checked.

- Fonts meet minimum size requirements.

- Plain Talk applied: short sentences, clear headings, active voice,
  common words.

- No unexplained acronyms.

- Links, phone numbers, emails, names, titles and dates verified.

- Real member or employee data removed from examples/screenshots unless
  approved.

- Review route identified before final distribution.

# 10. Starter patterns for common assets

## One-page job aid layout

- Header: title + one-sentence purpose.

- Section 1: Who this is for.

- Section 2: Before you start.

- Section 3: Steps, numbered in order.

- Section 4: What good looks like / success check.

- Section 5: Help path.

- Footer: document owner, update date and licensee disclosure if company
  name appears.

## SharePoint news layout

- Banner: clean image, one headline, limited text.

- Lead: why this matters in one short paragraph.

- Action block: what employees need to do.

- Details: who, when, impact, training link and support path.

- FAQ: use real employee questions when possible.

- Close: next step or deadline.

## Screenshot callout standard

- Use realistic UI captures with fictional data.

- Crop to the task area while preserving enough context.

- Use Bluejay numbered markers for primary callouts.

- Use no more callouts than needed. Three is usually enough.

- Keep labels short: command name, tab name or action.

- Add alt text that explains the screen and purpose, not every visible
  detail.

## Deck slide rhythm

- Title slide: topic + date or audience.

- Why it matters: one business reason.

- What is changing: short explanation.

- How to do it: demo or steps.

- Practice / check for understanding.

- Help path and close.
