## Page 94 — Add a shared mailbox  
## Add a shared mailbox  
## Use this when  
Open an approved team or departmental mailbox in Outlook on the managed iPad.  
## Before you start  
Confirm that:  
* The shared mailbox already exists.  
* Access was approved.  
* You are signed in with the company account that received permission.  
* You know the correct mailbox name or email address.  
* You understand whether you may read, manage or send from it.  
Adding the mailbox does not grant permission. Access must be assigned separately.  
## Add the mailbox  
1. Open Outlook.  
2. Tap the account or profile control.  
3. Tap the **Add Account** control.  
4. Tap **Add a Shared Mailbox**.  
5. Enter the shared mailbox name or email address.  
6. Select the correct result.  
7. Wait for Outlook to add the mailbox.  
8. Tap the shared mailbox.  
9. Open **Inbox**.

## Page 98 — Reply and prevent duplicate responses  
## Reply from the shared mailbox  
## Use this when  
Respond to a message received in Shared Inbox.  
## Before replying  
Check:  
* Has someone already responded?  
* Is another employee handling it?  
* Is an ownership category or status applied?  
* Does Shared Sent contain a recent response?  
* Is the conversation current?  
* Is the shared account selected?  
## Reply  
1. Open the message.  
2. Review the full conversation.  
3. Check Shared Sent Items.  
4. Confirm ownership.  
5. Tap **Reply** or **Reply All**.  
6. Confirm the shared sending account.  
7. Review the recipients.  
8. Write the response.  
9. Update the team status.  
10. Tap **Send**.  
## Suggested team statuses  

| Status        | Meaning                        |
| ------------- | ------------------------------ |
| New           | Not reviewed                   |
| In progress   | Someone is handling it         |
| Waiting       | Waiting for information        |
| Ready to send | Final review is required       |
| Complete      | Response sent or work closed   |
| Escalated     | Additional support is required |
  
Use the team’s established labels when they already exist.  
## Prevent duplicate replies  
Before sending:  
1. Review the conversation.  
2. Check Shared Sent.  
3. Review the team status.  
4. Confirm who owns the work.  
5. Update the status to In progress when required.  
6. Send only when ownership is clear.  
## What to remember  
Unread does not mean unassigned.

* Do not assume another employee has not responded.  
* Do not use Reply All unless everyone needs the response.  
* Do not leave completed work marked In progress.

## Page 104 — Verify the thread summary  
## Verify the summary before acting  
Review every important statement against the source conversation.  

| Check         | Question                                     |
| ------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| Topic         | Does the summary describe the correct issue? |
| Decision      | Was a decision actually confirmed?           |
| Action        | Is the requested task accurate?              |
| Owner         | Was someone explicitly assigned?             |
| Deadline      | Was the date stated and agreed to?           |
| Context       | Is an important condition missing?           |
| Latest update | Did the summary include the newest message?  |
  
****Verification process****  
1. Read the summary.  
2. Identify important statements.  
3. Open cited messages when available.  
4. Compare each statement with the source.  
5. Note missing or uncertain information.  
6. Identify the actual next action.  
7. Make the follow-up visible.  
## Example  
**Summary says:** The team agreed to complete the update by Friday.  
**Conversation says:** Friday was suggested but not confirmed.  
**Correct interpretation:** The deadline is proposed.  
**Next action:** Ask the owner to confirm the date.  
## Make follow-up visible  
After verifying, choose:  
* Flag  
* Snooze  
* To Do  
* Calendar event  
* Clarification reply  
* Category  
## What to remember  
Missing information creates a follow-up need. Do not fill the gap with an assumption.  
## Avoid this  
* Do not turn a proposed date into a commitment.  
* Do not assign ownership that is not stated.  
* Do not ignore contradictions.  
* Do not stop after generating the summary.
